  2. Study summary

    3. This study is concerned with the feasibility of dynamic touchscreen keyboards. This is important because we live in an age where mobile technology plays a large role in our daily lives. This study aims to see if they can be made easier to use through automatic adaptation to each individual user. I am seeking participants who are aged 18+ and use a smartphone or tablet on a daily basis. Participation in the research would require you to open a webpage on a mobile device and try a few different keyboards, followed by some opinions on each of them, this will take approximately 30 minutes of your time.

  3. What is the purpose of the study?

    4. This study aims to see if typing speed and accuracy on mobile devices can be improved by automatically adapting the sizes and positions of keys to the user's input patterns. Be it where the user touches each key or how frequently the user makes a consistent typing error.

  4. Why have I been invited?

    5. You may have come across this study via a link shared across the internet. People of all ages over 18 from any background are welcome to participate.

  5. Do I have to take part?

    6. No, taking part in this research is entirely voluntary. It is up to you to decide if you want to volunteer for the study. We will describe the study in this information sheet. If you agree to take part, we will then ask you to sign the attached consent form, dated 11/4/23, version number, 1.0.

  6. What will happen to me if I take part?

    7. The study will present you with two keyboards, one will be a standard keyboard, that will be used as the control for data collection. The other will be a dynamic keyboard, which will change the positions and sizes of keys based on your hit points when any errors are made as you type. A practice session will precede the data gathering sessions to allow you to get familiar with the keyboard beforehand. The practice session will display borders around the keys to make you aware of the default hit boxes for each key; these borders will be removed during the data gathering session to remove variables that may introduce bias. You will be asked to type some pangram sentences using these keyboards three times across two sessions, before being given the other keyboard to repeat the task. The keyboards will be presented to you at random to avoid any biases that may occur as you use them.

    9. After you have participated in the study, you do not need to do anything else.

  9. What data will be collected and / or measurements taken?

    10. Upon completion of the experiment, your average typing speed for each test will be recorded alongside any typing errors that you may have made. Any opinions of the tests will also be collected. This data will be used to determine the effectiveness of the test keyboard developed in this study. No personally identifiable information will be collected.

  10. What are the possible disadvantages, burdens or risks of taking part?

  11. What are the possible advantages or benifits of taking part?

    12. Researching a better keyboard is beneficial because soft keyboards play a daily role in our digital lives. A more efficient and ergonomic keyboard can aid in faster text entry and less errors when performing common tasks such as messaging and searching the internet. Further benefits can be had as well, including greater accessibility for those who have dexterity inhibiting diseases such as arthritis.
